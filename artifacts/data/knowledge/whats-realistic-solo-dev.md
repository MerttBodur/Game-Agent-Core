# What's Realistic for a Solo Developer

## Scope vs. Reality

Solo developers and small teams (1–3 people) operate under hard constraints: limited hours, limited budget, and no specialisation redundancy. A realistic scope targets a single mechanic or experience loop, not a genre-defining sandbox.

## What a Beginner Can Ship

**In weeks (2–8):** A small 2D game with one mechanic — a platformer level, a simple puzzle game, a Pong/Snake clone, a text adventure, a top-down shooter with limited enemies. Asset packs and AI generation tools close the art gap fast.

**In months (3–6):** A polished short game (30–120 min playtime), a mobile hyper-casual title, a small narrative/visual novel, a single-mode strategy game.

**In 1–2 years (part-time):** A full indie title with multiple levels, a roguelite with meaningful content variety, a small multiplayer prototype.

## How Budget Affects Scope

- **$0–$500:** Free engines (Godot, Unity Personal), AI art tools (freemium), free/CC0 assets. Targets mobile or itch.io.
- **$500–$5,000:** Licensed asset packs, Unreal Fab marketplace, paid AI subscriptions (Meshy Pro, Suno). Still solo-viable for mid-scope games.
- **$5,000+:** Can hire contractors for art, audio, or QA. Scope can expand to a commercial indie release.

## Unrealistic Asks (Block These)

The following combinations signal scope that a solo developer cannot deliver in the described timeline — flag them as infeasible:

- **GTA-scale open world** built by 1–2 people in weeks or months. Requires hundreds of person-years.
- **Photorealistic open world** with zero art capability and a sub-$1,000 budget. Photorealistic art production alone exceeds this budget by orders of magnitude.
- **Persistent online MMO** as a first solo project. Requires dedicated server infrastructure, anti-cheat, balance, and community management — none of which a solo beginner can sustain.
- **AAA-quality 3D action game** in under a year solo, with no prior engine experience.
- **Multiplayer FPS** with ranked matchmaking built solo in under six months.

When any of these patterns is detected, the feasibility gate must block and explain why before recommending tools.
